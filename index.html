Voters of Wisconsin

This map gives a detailed view of where Wisconsin voters are and who they voted for in the 2014 election for governor—the most recent election with widespread voter turnout. In this election, Republican Scott Walker won over Democrat Mary Burke, with Walker taking 52% of the votes compared to Burke's 47%.

Of Wisconsin's 4.3 million eligible voters, roughly 2.4 million went to the polls in this election. This level of voter turnout is comparable to that of the last election for president in 2012 and dwarfs the turnout of the two most recent Spring elections in which less that a million voters went to the polls.

Milwaukee and its suburbs represent roughly a quarter of the state's eligible voters. It is also one of the most racially segregated cities in United States. The Democrat-Republican divide closely follows the racial divide of the city. The urban center of the city represents one-fifth of all Democrats in the state, while the surrounding suburbs represent roughly one-fifth of all Republicans. Voter turnout also differs sharply between urban and suburban Milwaukee. While just over half of eligible urban voters cast a vote, over two-thirds of eligible suburban voters went to the polls.

Wisconsin's capital and second largest city, Madison, is a stronghold for Democrats. Dane county, the encompassing county of Madison, represents over 15% of the state's Democrats and had the second highest Democrat-voting percentage behind the Menominee Indian Reservation.
